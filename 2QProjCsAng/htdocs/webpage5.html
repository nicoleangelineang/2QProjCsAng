NFTs: In the Flesh

Some well-known examples of NFTs

In this section, we will be discussing two of the leading - Visual Art and Music - and two of the most promising - In-game Items and Online Items - NFT domains.

Visual Art

Visual Art is usually mistaken for the only type of NFT to exist, especially because its transaction mirrors that of the traditional art collecting that art enthusiants do through museums and exhibits, and while it is, indeed, the most common type that exists on the market, it is only one of the many. As of currently, the three most valuable NFT tokens are of the type visual art, and there are hundreds more being sold every day, making it a very promising market for visual artists.

The Most Expensive Visual
Art NFT sold
(one owner)

"Everydays: the First 5000 Days" is a digital work by Beeple that ended up being sold to one artist for a price of $69,346,250. Specifically, it is a collection of 5,000 digital artworks that Beeple made in a span of 5,000 days (one each day), stitched together with recurring themes - both the light and happy, as well as the dark illustrations - into one big collage. According to Beeple, the progression of the artworks reveal his growth as an artist.

The Most Expensive Visual Art NFT sold
(multiple owners)

"The Merge" is a digital artwork by Pak that ended being sold to multiple owners for a price of approximately $91,800,000. Specifically, 28,000 buyers bought 266,445 units of "The Merge" which could be then totalled up to the price mentioned. The work is of two white planets seemingly in the process of merging.

Music

Music and Audio as NFTs are still a relatively new domain that is being explored by musicians and artists, but because of the success of artists like Steve Aoki and Grimes, its market has been on a steady growth throughout the last year. Its main advantage for musicians is that, while they are usually unable to earn 100% of the profit for traditional marketplaces due to record labels and contracts, they are able to do this in NFTs because of its nature.

In-game Items

The gaming industry is one of the biggest industries now in this modern age, and since its expansion to the NFT marketplace, in-game collectibles (specifically, their ownership records) such as models, skins and in-game currency can be bought by and for interested buyers. Some companies also offer limited editions, giving its playerbase more incentive to buy a limited file of an already limited asset. And with majority of the profitable games being digital, game developers can easily jump on the bandwagon and grow their in-game collectible demand and economy.

The largest NFT game in the Philippines

AxIe Infinity is currently one of the most popular crypto games ever made, and it is currently the largest in the Philippines. One of its characters, the Angel AxIe, considered one of the strongest at level Mystic, has been sold at 300 ETH, for a price of approximately $1,100,000.

The first NFT Game and its Kitties

"Dragon", Kitty #896775, a pale purple dragon-type kitty, was sold for a price of 600 ETH, which is currently $2,300,000 now. "Dragon" is a character from the NFT Game Cryptokitties where people can buy colorful cats at different prices.

Online Items

With NFTs being at the early stages still of its success as a digital market, the sky is currently the limit as more and more people look for anything of value - tweets, tiktokss, articles, and etc. And while these currently aren't a big portion of the market, it is growing in numbers and will no doubt be interestig to witness in the next year.

First Tweet Sold as an NFT

Twitter CEO, Jack Dorsey, on October 24, 2021, sold his tweet as an NFT for the price of $2,900,000. Specifically, the tweet in question was the first tweet in the history of Twitter, which is now also the first tweet to be sold as an NFT
