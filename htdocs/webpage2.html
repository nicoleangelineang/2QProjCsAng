NFTs: The New (Bad) Big Thing

What are they? How do they Work?


In this world and age, it’s hard to keep up with trends when everything has gone too high technology for the majority. This also means that, by extension, the creations and their purposes have begun to become more and more hard to understand. NFTs are an example of these, and because of the explosion of blockchain technology, it’s become bigger than ever and people need to start understanding what it is to become more aware of the world today.

So, what is an NFT? And what’s all the hype on it about?

DEFINITION

NFTs stand for Non-fungible tokens
that is, it is a blockchain-based unique digital piece token on a blockchain, usually images of art, music, or digital representations of assets like collectibles - cards, rarities, etc. with unique identification codes and metadata that allow them to be unique from all other tokens.

This means that every single NFT is unique and there exists no other token like it.



HOW ARE THEY TRADED?

They are primarily traded using Ethereum, a cryptocurrency, through a blockchain wherein the seller will receive Ethereum and the buyer will receive the NFT.
However, the token itself is not a cryptocurrency, because they cannot be traded or exchanged for commercial transactions. Additionally, they are extensible, meaning that multiple NFTs can be combined to create a third NFT that is just as unique.


EXPLAINING BLOCKCHAINS

By definition,
a blockchain is a database system that stores information digitally akin to a digital ledger. It makes it so that the information stored is impossible to hack or alter, though accessible.

Its importance is,

to ensure transparency, security, and traceability of digital transactions, usually for cryptocurrency, without third party individuals

NFTs, and most cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin, Ethereum, and etc. use the blockchain technology. It is especially important because blockchain operates with no one in charge - that is, it allowed a decentralized cryptocurrency where owners were unable to alter their client’s currencies and files unlike before where clients had to merely trust the creators to not tamper with it.

HOW DOES IT WORK?

Blockchains work in a few steps that ensure security.



1 Transaction is recorded
2 Transaction is transmitted to designated high-end computers across the network
3 The designated computers work to confirm the transaction's validity
4 Once confirmed legitimate, all information is clustered into a block
5 When further transactions are done, they are chained to the rest to create a list of history. Hence, it is called a blockchain

this ensures that all blocks in the blockchain are being verified but at the same time, not being handled by third party individuals.
